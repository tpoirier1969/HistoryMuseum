Drury and Dixon Liberated

	Lieut. Alfred Drury, son of Dr. and Mrs. C. P. Drury, 414 East Hewitt avenue, and Lieut. Jack J. Dixon, husband of Mrs. Patrica Kepler Dixon, 104 East Magnetic street, have been liberated from Stalag Luft 1, German prison camp at Barth on the Baltic, according to telegrams the families received from the Red Cross.
	Lieut. Drury, a co-pilot on a B-25   bomber,   was   taken   prisoner December 2, 1943, when his plane vent down  in  Italy on  his 22nd nission. Four other crew members vere killed, but the status of the fifth is unknown. Lieut Drury was first taken to a classification camp at.  Dresden,   and   later  moved  to Stalag 1, where 7,000 officers and 2,000 enlisted men were imprisoned. 
	Entering the Army in April, 1942, he went overseas in August, 1943.
	Before entering the air corps, he took the flight training course offered to civilians at the Northern Michigan College of Education.
	Prior to receiving the Red Cross telegram, the Drurys' had last heard from their son on December 17. During the 17 months he was a prisoner, they received eight messages from him.
	Dr. and Mrs. Drury believe their son is now in England, because of radio and press information recently released regarding the liberation of the prison camp May 2 by the Russians. It was further announced that the prisoners were being evacuated to England by bombers.
	Lieut. Dixon had been a prisoner of the Germans since a year ago April. He likewise was a pilot in the air corps, and has been overseas about 17 months.
