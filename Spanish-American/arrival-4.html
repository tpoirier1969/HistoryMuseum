Clara’s Story, part 1/3

Clara Lambert’s husband, Joseph, died of pulmonary tuberculosis in 1921 at Morgan Heights hospital in Negaunee, Michigan. A veteran of the Spanish American War, Joseph believed his illness was caused by exposure to malaria during his service in Cuba. Born in 1877 in Quebec Canada, Joseph Lambert immigrated to Ironwood, Michigan. He was not yet an American citizen when he joined the Michigan National Guard. He served in Cuba with Co. E. 34th Regiment Michigan Infantry. Upon his return home, he was treated for “malarial poisoning” by a local doctor yet he never fully regained his health. In 1916, he moved to Marquette, Michigan with his wife and three young children. A trained mason, Joseph had difficulty earning a living after the war due to chronic health issues.

His widowed wife Clara and three children were left with a large debt and no property of value. Clara’s widow’s pension provided $12 a month and $2 per child. In order to secure a higher monthly benefit, she needed to prove her husband’s disease was caused by his exposure to malaria years earlier.
