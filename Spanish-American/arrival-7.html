From Manual of Laws, Relating to Pensions

Pension is granted by the general laws to all persons disabled by reason of any wound or injury received or disease contracted in line of duty while in the military or naval service of the United States at any time.

The disability resulting from disease there are five general grades of pension per month, as follows: Rate varies with rank from $8-$30.

Specific Rates for disabilities
Loss of both hands $100.00
Total disability in both hands; loss of both feet; loss of both eyes; loss of one eye, if the other was lost before enlistment $72.00
Total disability of arm or leg; loss of one hand and foot; total disability in one hand and foot; amputation at or above elbow or knee. $36
Loss of hand or foot; total disability of one hand or one foot; total deafness $30
For disability requiring the regular aid and attendance of another person…. $72
For disability requiring the frequent but not regular attendance of another person…. $50
For total inability to perform any manual labor…$30
