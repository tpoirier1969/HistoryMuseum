War Begins

“Yellow Journalism” had its start in the Spanish American War. Newspapers were the primary source for information in the country at the time. Editors often offered their interpretation of the news over objective journalism. If the facts presented were distorted, the public had few options for fact checking. Flamboyant headlines increased circulation and this irresponsible approach to journalism became know as “yellow journalism.”

William Randolph Hearst and Joseph Pulitzer lead the charge for American involvement in Cuba. Their competing publications reported alleged brutality of the Spanish toward Cuban rebels. By the time the USS Maine exploded in Havana Harbor, the pro-war press made it difficult for President McKinley to make any other choice but to enter the war. His political future rested upon that.
