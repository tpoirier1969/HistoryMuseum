Upper Peninsula Communities supported Soldiers

Living conditions in Cuba and the other areas affected by the war were miserable for the local boys of Company L. When letters began arriving from the front, local individuals began fundraising for the soldiers. Lawn socials were held and chain letters were sent to raise dollars for the effort. At the same time, Ishpeming people began collecting clothes and others prepared relief “care” packages to be sent to local boys. By the time the local effort was organized and packages were sent, the war was nearly over.
