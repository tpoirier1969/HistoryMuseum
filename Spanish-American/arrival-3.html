Caring for Veterans and Family after War

After returning home, Spanish American veterans could apply for a pension from the federal government. The veterans’ assistance program was expanded to include benefits and pensions not only to veterans but also their widows and dependents. Soldiers killed in battle as well as veterans who had served their country were eligible for burial in a federal or nationally funded cemetery.

At the turn of the century the pension program reached almost half the federal spending budget. This is due to veterans and widows living longer and an increase in fraudulent claims. As a result, legislation was put in place that required individuals to provide evidence directly connecting war service to disability or illness. This was especially difficult for veterans whose disabilities were a result of exposure to an unfamiliar disease, new environmental hazards and the emotional trauma of battle. Their symptoms didn’t present themselves until years later, making it difficult to find evidence required to claim benefits.
