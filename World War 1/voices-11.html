Movement Home
12 November 1918 - 23 May 1919

The Great War, the war to end all wars, World War I, after more then four bloody years has come to an end. The 32nd Division moved to the American Occupation Sector near Colbenz in December 1918 and remained there until April 1919, On April 18, 1919 after completing it occupation duties in Germany, the division moved to Brest France. Beginning on 20 April units of the division began their trip home. The last of the division's troops arrived in New York on 22 May, The 64rd Infantry Brigade demobilized at Camp Grant Illinois on 17 May and the 63th infantry Brigade and division troops demobilized at Camp Custer Michigan on 23 May. The division's epic journey during the Great War had come to an end. During the war, the division had fought in three major offensives. Aisne- Marne, Oise - Aisne, and the Meuse - Argonne, suffered over 14,000 casualties, and was the first American unit to set foot on German soil. The men of the 32™* Division, “Les Terribles" returned home to well earned displays of appreciation for their service and sacrifice.
