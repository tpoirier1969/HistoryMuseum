Mobilizing the Troops

When the United States entered World War in Aprii of 1917 American industry was not prepared to meet the needs of the American military. In order to get the American Expeditionary Force in battle as soon as possible, the AEF would have to be equipped with mostly British and French weapons and equipment. All of the tanks used by the AEF were provided by the French as well as most of the light and heavy machine guns. Of the 3,500 artillery pieces used by the AEF in France, over 3,000 were British or French. Of the 2,698 air planes flown by the Army Air Service over 2,000 were provided by the French or British. But what the Allies needed most was what American provided the most of, 1.8 million Doughboys. With the Allied armies bled dry after three years of horrendous casualties, only the Unites States could provide the man power to defeat the Central Powers.
