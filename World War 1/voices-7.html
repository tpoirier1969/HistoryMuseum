The Alsace Sector

Between late February and the middle of May, the division performed support duties, obtained replacements and continued to train for combat. On 14 May the division moved to the vicinity of La Chapefle-sous-Rougemont in the 7th French Army sector. On the night of 20/21 May 1918, the Doughboys of the 32nd Division-lumberjacks, miners, farmers, shop keepers, railroad men and students from the cities, villages and farms of the U.P. entered the world of the trenches. Operating with the 9th and 10th French divisions in the Alsace sector, the Doughboys would receive their baptism of fire as they patrolled No Man's Land and raided German trenches. For the next two months, men from the U.P, like the millions of other men who went before them, would experience the misery of life in the trenches. During this Time, 55 men were killed and 202 wounded in the division.
