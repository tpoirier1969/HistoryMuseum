Gold Star Mothers

A Women’s Committee of National Defense was begun in 1918. They recommended that women wear a black band with a gold star on the upper left arm. This was suggested instead of the traditional mourning attire of the time.

The term “Gold Star Mother” was first used by President Wilson in a letter to the Women’s Committee. The star was intended to show the sacrifice of a family member as well as to provide a measure of pride and consolation. In 1928 a group of women made plans to begin the American Gold Star Mothers, Inc.

This organization began to take on additional responsibilities. In addition to promoting peace and good will, the organization: assisted veterans and dependents in presenting claims to the V.A., preserved the memory of those who sacrificed, taught patriotism and respect for the flag.

This organization is active today, providing support to veteran’s hospitals and attending veteran’s ceremonies. Today a small gold star pin is presented to the mother and often the other family members, by the U.S. Government.
