Air Warfare

Despite having given birth to the airplane in 1903, the U.S. was slow to explore the military applications of the airplane and had no air force when World War I began. By contrast Britain, France, Germany, Austria-Hungary and even Russia had useful air arms. AH sides understood the importance of aerial reconnaissance. By 1915 technological advancement in aircraft design, more powerful engines and ability to mount machine guns allow the armies to expand the roles of airplanes. Fighter planes now fought for air superiority over the battlefields, and bombers now dropped their deadly ioads of high explosives on the enemy. Most of the aircraft flown by the United States Army Air Service (USAAS) were provided by the French.
