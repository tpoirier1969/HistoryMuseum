First Casualties

The doughboys suffered their first casualties: three were killed and eleven captured when the Germans conducted a trench raid on their position. Throughout the winter of 1917 and the spring of 1918, American units would continue their training with the Allies and take over portions of the Allies' trench to sharpen their combat skills. In April 1918, a German attack on the American trenches near Seicheprey France left over 650 American killed and wounded and 100 taken prisoner. It was a costly learning experience for the Doughboys, but they quickly learned the skills they would need to take the fight to the Germans. In the great battles of the fall of 1918, the AEF would leave the trenches, going on the offense and bringing about the final deft of the enemy and an end to the Great War.
