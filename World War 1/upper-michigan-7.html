Native Americans

Native American people who served in the U.S. armed forces are greatly honored in the American Indian Community. The translation for soldier, warrior, protector and helper are all the same word. 

In Ojibwe it is Ogichida. 

It is a remarkable fact that Indian people served the U.S. long before they were even given United States citizenship. In WWI between 1917 and 1918, over 10,000 American Indian people enlisted in the armed services. Native Americans were granted the right to vote in 1924.
