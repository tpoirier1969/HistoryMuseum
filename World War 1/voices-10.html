The Meuse - Argonne Operation
September 26 –November 11, 1918

The division's short break from combat was soon over as it was ordered to join the American First Army for what would become the division's longest campaign of the war. Carrying over seventy pounds of equipment, the soldiers of the division marched over 11 miles on the dark, rainy and cold night of 30 September to reach their position near Cierges. On 4 October the division attacked towards Gesnes and continued the attack on the 5th, capturing Bois de la Morine, Bois du Chene-Sec and Gesnes after intense hand to hand fighting. In heavy combat on 9 October the division attacked and captured Mamelle Trench and on 10 October the division cleared the enemy from the Fme du Transvaal, The division was not poised to attack the main German defensive position, the Kriemhiide Stellung. On 14 October the division again attacked, breaking through the defenses of the Kriemhiide Line and capturing Romagne-sous-Montfaucon Cote Dame-Marie and the eastern portion of Bois de Gesnes. On 15 and 16 October the division again attacked, fighting their way into the Bois de Chauvignon and the eastern part of the Bois de Romagne. The division continued to advance through 19 October and on 20 October it was pulled from the lines. On 10 November the division crossed the Meuse and enters the lines near Breheville and pursued fleeing enemy forces. On 11 November, which will later become known as Armistice Day, the division holds the line near the village of Peuvillers. This last campaign which saw the "Les Terribles" break through the Hindenburg Line cost the division 1179 men killed and 4407 wounded.
