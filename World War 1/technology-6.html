Submarines

Large scale use of submarines against surface ships began during World War i. While both sides used submarines to sink enemy ships, the German Navy's Untersee Boot or U Boats were the most successful. The Rules of War required submarines to warn merchant ships prior to attacking them. As Royal Navy anti-submarine measures increased during the war, the Germans responded with a campaign of "unrestricted submarine warfare" (sinking ships without warning).
