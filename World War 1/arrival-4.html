Ishpeming's  World War I Victory Arch

Bell ringing was used to welcome glad news such as the end of World War I. When the Armistice was signed in November of 1918, news reached the U.S. in the early morning. In Marquette, the AP news service contacted the Mining Journal which contacted the mayors of Negaunee, Ishpeming and Marquette at 2:00 am. They decided to spread the good news around 4:00 am. Newspaper boys got people out of bed with their cries of “Extra!” Ishpeming was reported to have set the local record for the largest celebration. The bells of the fire hall and churches were kept ringing for hours. Men, women and children lined up for the privilege of ringing the fire hall bell. The railroad men kept the whistles blowing.
