Federal Pensions

The Civil War saw a big expansion in federal pensions. Pensions were in part a way to encourage volunteers. The amount a veteran or widow received depended both on rank and level of disability. With advances in medicine, the war allowed for more wounded to survive resulting in more veterans in need of care.

The custom of providing for our veterans dates back to early colonial laws. The first National Pension Law was enacted in 1776. It offered half a year’s wage to severely disabled veterans. Over time legislation expanded to include widows and orphans. The Civil War brought the greatest change to veterans and their families. In 1862, one year after the start of the war, the General Pension Act increased benefits, expanded eligibility, and made it easier to make claims.
