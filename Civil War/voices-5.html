July 1863

“Because of the continual rain the company slept in a church. As far as your humble servant was concerned, he had no fault to find what ever with his bed. Having had the pleasure before in times gone past of sleeping with a crowd, I couldn't see the propriety of indulging myself this time, and as the altar was unoccupied save by the large Bible generally found there, I concluded the place was good enough for me, so I took possession. The Bible and my two revolvers made a pretty good pillow, my horse blanket folded once made a soft bed and my coat over my shoulders made a good coverlid. I debated quite a while within myself as to whether I would remove my boots or not, but after considering the pros and cons all over, I came to the conclusion that if I should oversleep myself and someone should accidently happen to walk off in my boots that I should have to make the remainder of the trip barefoot. So for that reason I slept with them on."
