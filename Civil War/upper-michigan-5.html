Feelings Ran High

"Feelings ran high and 'The Union Forever' was on every tongue. In all the community there was but one rebel sympathizer, a Mr. Moss, who was an agent at the Delaware1 He often showed disdain for the North and, when the men strung a Union banner above and across the road, he would make a detour through the woods rather than pass under the starry flag. The men taunted him so much that he finally left the country."
