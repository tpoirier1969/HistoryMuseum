Crossing the Rapidan and Battle of the Wilderness

“We crossed the Rapidan [River] on the 5th and on the 6th took part in the battle of Wilderness. This wilderness is a large tract of land covered with small trees and bushes principally fine/ and in places so dense as to prevent a passage through. We came upon the enemy about noon, connected our line, and, seeing a regiment in front, Major Moody gave orders not to fire until he gave the command. Our skirmishers were sent forward and had advanced but a few rods when this regiment in front poured a volley into our ranks, and then we saw they were rebels dressed in our clothes, and we paid them back with interest. The battle continued with great fury all the afternoon. About 3 o'clock P.M. the line of battle was formed [again] and we advanced on the rebels and drove them from two lines of breastworks. As we advanced our line was broken up and when our regiment halted we found ourselves almost alone there being few other regiments with us, the enemy seeing us thus unsupported and far in advance marched a column on our flank compelling us to fall back to a position a little in advance of where we started. Another charge was attempted later in the afternoon but the enemy having been reinforced, we could not dislodge them. The result of this day's fighting was highly gratifying to our side, the ememy having been driven at all points, instead of driving us as [General] Lee intended to do.
