Railroad Trains

The recent technology of railways became an important part of the war. Troops, materials and food were all moved, as well as materials for civilians to support the war effort. For this reason, they were often targeted. The South used trains to quickly move support troops to a battle. Eventually the North began to use rails, which it had more of and of a more consistent gauge. General Sherman trained 10,000 troops to repair tracks, so they could be back in use after 1 or 2 days. When Sherman’s troops marched to Savannah, they pulled up ties and rails, set them on fire, then bent and twisted rails around trees. This crippled the South.
