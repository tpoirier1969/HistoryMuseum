USS Michigan Warship in Marquette

USS Michigan at the Spear Dock in Lower Harbor, commissioned in 1844; it was the first iron hulled ship of the US Navy. Rumors of confederate plots in Northern cities, brought this Civil War ship to Sault Ste. Marie, Marquette and Houghton. In Marquette and Houghton, the sailors broke up the striking miners. While copper and iron prices doubled during the war, miners’ wages did not. Following the war, salaries and production began to drop leading the miners to strike. Area citizens began to panic when mining equipment and property were destroyed, railroad lines were torn up and rumors were heard of burning Marquette. When the USS Michigan arrived at Marquette’s docks, Lt Commander Francis Roe armed the 130 sailors with pistols, rifles and two howitzer guns, and they rode railcars to nearby mining camps. 

The commander told the miners to return to work within 24 hours or the “encampment would be stormed by shot and shell and no questions would be asked or answered. There must be no more rioting, no more idleness and no more threats.” The miners returned to work and several days later the warship headed to Houghton to face similar circumstances.
