GAR Forms in Upper Michigan

In Upper Michigan, a group called the Soldiers and Sailors Association of the Upper Peninsula began to organize in 1879. Representatives came from Baraga, Houghton, Keweenaw, Marquette and Ontonagon Counties. Members from there formed the GAR posts E.R. Stiles Post in the Copper Country and the Alfred Jackson Post began in Marquette in 1885. By joining the GAR, they hoped to bring more political leverage to their cause.
