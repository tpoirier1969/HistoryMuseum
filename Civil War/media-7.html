Brady’s photo outfit Petersburg, VA

During the Civil War, photography was complex and time consuming. Two photographers would arrive at a location. One would mix chemicals and pour them on a clean glass plate. After the chemicals were given time to evaporate, the glass plate would be sensitized by being immersed in darkness in a bath solution. The plate was then inserted into the camera, positioned and focused. The two had to complete the exposure and development within ten minutes.

Next, the exposed plate was rushed to a wagon darkroom for developing. All this quick and careful handling of the glass plate was done on a battlefield.
