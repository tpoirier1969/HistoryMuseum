John B. LaVake

John LaVake served in Sherman’s march to the sea. He enlisted at 22 in 1862. His last battle was Atlanta, and he was discharged in 1865.   He was raised in Sault Ste. Marie and resettled in St. Ignace. His grandfather was a tribal leader who signed the 1825 Prairie du Chien treaty. He served as color bearer of the Fuller Post and died at 89.
