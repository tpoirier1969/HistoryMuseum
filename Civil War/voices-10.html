The Ojibwe - First Michigan Sharpshooters

Garrett A. Gravaraet (a metis) recruited many Ottawa and Ojibwe. Most of the men serving in Co. K were Indians from Michigan. Almost 2,000 of the 7,755 men who served were from the Upper Peninsula. While patriotism and loyalty to the US were invoked in their call for service, the reason for service seems to relate to their need to develop trust and leverage in maintaining their lands in Michigan. At the time, Michigan was rapidly being settled. Even with treaty agreements, attempts had been made to move the Ottawa to the west of Mississippi.
The Regiment was involved in intense fighting from Spotsylvania to Crater, suffering great losses and being cited for heroism.
