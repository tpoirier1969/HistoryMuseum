Civil War Media

Dogtags

American soldiers during the Civil War were concerned that their identities be known should they die on the battlefield. There was no official form of identification such as “dog tags” so it was up to each man to identify himself. Some soldiers wrote their names and unit numbers on paper tags and pinned them to their uniforms. Others fashioned a tag out of wood which could be worn on a string around the neck. Harper’s Weekly magazine advertised “Soldiers Pins” which could be ordered and worn. They were inscribed with the soldier’s name and unit. Private vendors followed the troops and offered similar items for sale before major battles.
