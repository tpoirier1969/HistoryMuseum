The GAR grew into a social and political force around the country. By 1890, it had about half a million members across the nation. The Albert Jackson Post Memorial Book lists 107 members, some of whom enlisted from other areas and settled here later.

Eleven GAR members in Marquette with President Theodore Roosevelt (here for the Newett libel trial) in 1913: Mason W. Burt, William A. Jellison, Gustav Bahrman, Allen Cowden, Addison Cole, Edward C. Anthony, Courtland Moore, Anton "Cap" Kruger, Robert P. Byrne, Sam P. Byrne at Peter White/George Shiras III home on Ridge St.
